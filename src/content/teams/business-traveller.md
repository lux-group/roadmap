initiatives_section_title: "Key Initiatives"
key_initiatives:
  - heading: "Self Service Flight Cancellation"
    description: "Enable travellers to cancel their booked flights directly through the LEBT website without needing to contact customer service. Initially scoped for Virgin and Qantas carriers as they are supplied by Sabre. This self-service capability reduces operational overhead on the customer support team and gives travellers more autonomy over their bookings, while keeping all changes recorded within the platform for accurate reporting and audit trails."
  - heading: "Item Policies and Approval Workflows"
    description: "Evolve the policy system to support granular, traveller-level assignment with many-to-many relationships between policies and employees. This enables businesses to tailor budget limits and booking parameters for different employee levels or departments. Rework the approval policy architecture to use proper relational models instead of flat arrays, supporting individual, group, and global policy assignment with defined precedence rules. These foundations unlock group-based management at scale, faster onboarding, and streamlined approval workflows."
  - heading: "Single Sign-On (SSO) for Business Clients"
    description: "Enable Single Sign-On via Microsoft Entra ID, allowing employees to log into LEBT using their organization's corporate identity provider. The login flow redirects employees to their company's configured identity provider for authentication, reducing login friction and leveraging the organization's existing security policies including multi-factor authentication and conditional access. SSO is a prerequisite for larger organizations evaluating enterprise travel management platforms and removes the need for LEBT-specific credentials."
